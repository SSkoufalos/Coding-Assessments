My Favorite Pokemon!


Hailing from the early days of Red and Blue, it's Porygon, Pokemon #137!


With Pokemon Red being my first proper Game Boy game, and Porygon being one of the harder species to obtain, the little cyber bird caught my attention early on with its hefty 9,999 coin price tag as a Game Corner prize. Even then, I had no idea that it was even related to programming code - it looked like a crystal creature, and I was enamored with it. Upon finally getting it and learning more about its intricacies, it became a mainstay of my team in spite of its then-lackluster stats - it was more of a pet than a fighter, but it did passably well, and I was happy with it. For better or worse, the little guy was here to stay.


Suffice to say, it wasn't until years later that I learned why the little bird hadn't appeared in the anime; I'd spent many a day watching in the vain hopes of seeing it, but the episode that had featured it was related to the infamous incident that gave hundreds of children seizures (even though it was Pikachu that caused the incident, not Porygon), and unfortunately, outside of cameos, the cyber crane never truly got the love it deserved. Its future in the game, however, was another story - Porygon would get two evolutionary forms over the years (Porygon2 and Porygon-Z) both of which would become top players in the competitive scene, and both of which are adorable in their own ways as well.


I suppose it should come as no surprise that with such great love for a Pokemon made entirely out of programming code (to say nothing of growing up with an older brother who was remarkably skilled with computers and building several with him), I would end up in this particular field of creating things with code as well. Perhaps one day I can become proficient enough to actually help engineer an artificial creature like this and bring about the existence of something similar to a Porygon in the real world - even if it is a long way off. For now, though, I will focus on keeping my creations in cyberspace.
